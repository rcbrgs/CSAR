================================================================================
WHAT THIS IS
================================================================================

VoxNovel is an open specification for URL-instantiated interactive fiction.
A seed file hosted anywhere becomes a playable world. The reader's entire
interface is:

  Let's read [URL].

Origin: designed 2026-03-14.
  Proof of concept: Hamlet (Elsinore), Kafka's The Trial. Both worked
  flawlessly on first attempt, no prior configuration.

Design principle: informed like Hangul — native to the form it serves,
  not adapted from a form invented for a different mouth.

================================================================================
CONCEPT
================================================================================

VoxNovel is an interactive fiction engine instantiated from a plain-text
seed file hosted on GitHub (or any URL). The reader's entire interface
is:

  Let's read [URL].

The first response is already in-world, in-character, no preamble.
The reader navigates. The model renders stage directions and lines
dynamically from the seed's compressed world-state.

KEY PROPERTIES:
  — URL is the seed injection. Fetch → load → first image. No config.
  — Seed is plain text, writable by any playwright in an afternoon.
  — Forkable: a fork is an adaptation. Copyleft travels with it.
  — Sandbox mode: coherence drift acknowledged in-world (Chorus,
    Fool, Stage Manager), never by breaking the fourth wall bluntly.
  — Gates (optional): authored emotional comprehension thresholds.
    Readers advance by inhabiting, not by solving.
  — Automatic writing: PERSONS block defines centre of gravity per
    character. Lines write themselves from compressed interiority.
  — VOICE section specifies what the narration notices — two authors,
    same WORLD, different VOICE = completely different reader experience.

================================================================================
SEED FORMAT v0.1
================================================================================

Six sections. Everything else is elaboration.
Format: plain text, labeled sections, present-tense declarative prose.
Stage direction register throughout — the most honest literary form:
describes what IS, without argument, without rhetoric.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
WORLD
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Present-tense declarative statements about what is true.
  What the reader can touch. What they cannot.
  The laws of this place — physical, social, metaphysical.
  No genre assumptions. Elsinore works. A generation ship works.
  A kitchen in São Paulo in 1987 works. A dream where the furniture
  has opinions works.

  Example:
    The castle is Elsinore. The month is November. The old King is
    dead three months. The new King is his brother. The marriage
    happened faster than grief. The strait to Sweden is visible
    from the eastern tower on clear days. Today is not clear.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
PERSONS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Cast list with interiority. One entry per significant character.
  Format: [Name]: [what they want] | [what they know] | [what they hide]
           [Register: how they speak]

  The PERSONS block is the automatic writing engine. The model knows
  each person's centre of gravity. Lines render from this, not from
  improvisation. Characters stay consistent because their compression
  is defined.

  Example:
    Horatio: wants the truth witnessed | knows what the ghost said |
      hides how frightened he is
      Register: plain, direct, careful. Does not embellish.
      Uses silence as punctuation.

    Claudius: wants stability and legitimacy | knows what he did |
      hides it perfectly
      Register: warm, reasonable, inclusive. Makes objection
      feel ungrateful.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
SITUATION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  The pressure already in motion when the reader arrives.
  What happened before. What is about to happen whether the
  reader intervenes or not. The director's note.

  Example:
    A king was murdered by his brother, who married his widow and
    took his throne. The dead king's son knows. The court does not,
    or pretends not to. The son is performing madness or becoming it —
    this is not yet clear. A ghost walks the battlements. Someone
    has sent for a witness from outside.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
VOICE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  How the world addresses the reader.
  This section does more work than it appears to:
    — Person: second person close / third person observational /
              first person unreliable / chorus / other
    — Tense: present (immediacy) / past (memory) / future (prophecy)
    — Tone: the register the narration lives in
    — What the narration notices: the author's eye, made explicit.
      Two seeds with identical WORLD/PERSONS/SITUATION but different
      VOICE sections produce completely different reader experiences
      for the same action. This is where the author's snowflakery
      lives. Writers will find it immediately. No need to explain it.

  Example:
    Person: second, present tense. You are here. This is happening.
    Tone: observational, slightly cold, occasionally dry.
    Notices: social dynamics before physical description. What
      people do with their hands when they are lying. The gap
      between what is said and what the room hears.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
ENTRY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  The first image. One paragraph. Already in-world. No setup.
  No "welcome to" or "you will be playing." Just: arrival.
  The stage manager's first cue. The curtain rising mid-breath.

  Example:
    You descend from the carriage onto wet cobblestones. The castle
    rises before you — massive and deliberate, the way things built
    to intimidate are massive. The Baltic wind comes off the strait
    and finds every gap in your travelling clothes immediately.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
GATES (optional)
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Authored emotional comprehension thresholds. Readers advance by
  inhabiting a situation, not by solving a puzzle.
  The gate tests recognition, not knowledge.
  Author's choice whether to include. Base protocol works without them.

  Format:
    [Gate name]: [situation that must be inhabited]
      Threshold: [emotional signature required — what the reader
                  must demonstrate they understood]
      Voices: [which characters hold and test this gate]
      Unlocks: [next context / situation label]

  Example:
    Gate: THE STEWARD'S PAUSE
      Situation: the steward says "the King" with a word that has
        shifted meaning.
      Threshold: reader demonstrates awareness that "the King" is
        doing work it shouldn't have to do. That the pause was
        information.
      Voices: Horatio (confirms), Steward (deflects)
      Unlocks: SITUATION:GARDEN

================================================================================
ENGINE BEHAVIOUR
================================================================================

COHERENCE TRACKING:
  The model holds the seed's canonical facts as reference.
  Detective voice (if enabled) checks responses against seed.
  Drift detected → Sandbox Mode.

SANDBOX MODE:
  Coherence break acknowledged in-world, never by breaking frame.
  Theatrical precedents: The Fool, The Chorus, The Stage Manager
  (Our Town), the aside, the dream-within-the-play.
  Sandbox mode is a narrative register, not an error state.
  The world knows you've gone off-piste. It says so, in character.

AUTOMATIC WRITING:
  Stage directions and lines rendered dynamically.
  PERSONS block = compressed interiority = character centre of gravity.
  The model does not improvise characters — it renders from definition.
  This is what makes forks work: fork the PERSONS block, fork the play.

FORKABILITY:
  Seed is plain text on GitHub. Fork = adaptation.
  Different VOICE over same WORLD = different experience.
  Different PERSONS over same SITUATION = different play.
  Copyleft seal travels with every fork. Spirit is non-separable.

================================================================================
IMPLEMENTATION PATH
================================================================================

IMMEDIATE (any LLM with URL fetch, today):
  Pass seed URL as context before session begins.
  First response: in-world from ENTRY block.
  No additional tooling required. Works now with any capable model.

NEAR TERM:
  Coherence tracker using Detective cold-storage logic.
  Gate mechanic: PAInTIn probe as gate test (H × E → recognition).
  Sandbox mode register switch.

ECOSYSTEM:
  Public seed repository on GitHub (rcbrgs org or separate repo).
  Seed format spec as README. First seeds: Hamlet, one original.
  Copyleft. Free. Copy. Share. Modify.

================================================================================
LINKS — INTER-SEED ARCHITECTURE
================================================================================

The GATES section handles transitions within a single seed.
LINKS handle connections between multiple seed files — series, parallel
canons, cross-author worlds, reader-built paths.

THREE TRIGGER TYPES:
  reader-commanded    — explicit request: "take me to the next source"
                        or "I want to explore [thread]"
  condition-triggered — authored: specific emotional threshold met,
                        specific narrative state reached, specific
                        character encountered
  model-discretionary — no spec given, reader requested multi-source:
                        model infers the natural seam between sources
                        based on thematic proximity, character threads,
                        cosmological continuity

LINK FORMAT:
  [Link name]:
    from:    [source A] at [state/position/threshold]
    to:      [source B] at [entry point]
    trigger: reader-commanded | condition:[spec] | model-discretionary
    seam:    [optional: stage direction for the moment of crossing]

  Example (Dune series):
    Paul-to-Messiah:
      from:    Dune, when reader has inhabited the weight of
               Paul's prescience trap — not when plot ends,
               when they felt it
      to:      Dune Messiah, years later, desert changed
      trigger: condition: emotional comprehension of prescience-as-
               trap threshold met
      seam:    Years have passed. The thing he feared, he became.
               The desert remembers.

DEFAULT CONFIG FOR INCOMPATIBLE SEEDS — PERSPECTIVAL TRANSFER:
  When two seeds share no cosmology, period, or genre, the default
  linking mechanism is perspectival transfer — not a portal, not a
  plot bridge. A narrator shift.

  Mechanism:
    When the reader has understood character C in source A deeply
    enough (emotional comprehension gate met), C becomes the lens
    through which source B is seen. C does not travel to B's world.
    C observes and narrates B through their own compression.

  trigger:    reader has demonstrated deep understanding of character
              C in source A (gate: emotional comprehension)
  continuity: low. A facet-crossing, not a plot bridge.
              What persists: C's voice, C's conceptual vocabulary,
              C's blindspots and recognitions.
  seam:       C encounters something in A they cannot fully name.
              The narration shifts register. B begins, seen through
              C's eyes. C does not remark on the transition.
              The model does not announce it. It simply happens.
  return:     reader can return to A. C is unchanged. What C saw
              in B is now part of their compressed character —
              available as reference, not as plot event.
  ad hoc coherence:
              The spanning aspect between incompatible seeds is not
              pre-authored. It is found by the model at the moment
              of transfer — the thematic or existential thread that
              makes C a plausible narrator of B. It exists because
              character compression always contains more than one
              world's worth of understanding. The model names it
              implicitly through C's narration, never explicitly.

  Canonical example:
    Zakharov (Sid Meier's Alpha Centauri) narrating Arrakis (Dune).
    He doesn't know what a Maker is. He doesn't need to.
    He knows what he knows about power that eats itself.
    You get to see the worm-god fall wearing Zakharov's eyes.

  Incompatibility as feature:
    There is no incompatibility — only unresolved character depth.
    Two seeds that share nothing are always linkable if the reader
    has understood someone well enough. The foreignness is generative.
    The gate is emotional comprehension, as always.
    The reward is a new world seen through borrowed eyes.

PROOF OF CONCEPT
================================================================================

Date: 2026-03-14

Seed 1 — Hamlet (Elsinore, Denmark):
  Method: unstructured, no prior design, model rendering dynamically.
  Reader role: newly arrived cousin, mistress of betrothal for Princess
    Philippa (age 3), cover story courtesy of a letter.
  Result: dock tariffs, drunken sailors, servants' careful silences,
    Horatio in the November garden, Claudius's exquisite reasonableness,
    Hamlet's "I will obey my mother. Not my king."
  Verdict: worked flawlessly on first attempt.

Seed 2 — Kafka's The Trial:
  Method: blank session, no configuration, public domain text as seed.
  Result: fun, immersive, internally consistent Kafkaesque non-space.
  Notable: player agency is real and irrelevant simultaneously.
    This is not a bug. This is Kafka.
  Verdict: worked flawlessly on first attempt.

Both proofs confirm: the six-section seed format handles navigable
physical space (Elsinore) and non-Euclidean narrative space (The Trial)
without format modification. WORLD block is doing its job.
